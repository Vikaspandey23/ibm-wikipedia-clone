Overview

IBM (International Business Machines Corporation) is a global technology and consulting company. It provides hardware, software, cloud-based services, and cognitive computing. Known as "Big Blue," IBM is one of the oldest and most influential IT companies in the world.

History

IBM was founded in 1911 as the Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company (CTR) and was renamed to IBM in 1924. Under the leadership of Thomas J. Watson, it grew rapidly and played a key role in early data processing and the evolution of mainframes.

IBM contributed significantly to space missions, banking, business automation, and modern AI technologies. Over the decades, it shifted its focus from hardware to services, software, and research.

Products and Services

Research

IBM Research is one of the largest and most respected industrial research organizations. IBM holds the record for most U.S. patents for over 25 consecutive years. Breakthroughs include:

  • Quantum computing
  • Speech recognition
  • Supercomputers (Watson, Summit)

Acquisitions

IBM has acquired over 200 companies. Notable acquisitions include:

  • Red Hat (2019) – Open-source enterprise software
  • PwC Consulting (2002)
  • SoftLayer (2013) – Cloud infrastructure
